TCEQ License Types

Texas regulates water and wastewater professionals through the Texas Commission on Environmental Quality. Here's what each license means.

Water Treatment

Water Treatment Operators

Licensed to operate and maintain public water treatment systems including filtration, disinfection, and chemical treatment processes.

WW Treatment

Wastewater Treatment Operators

Licensed to operate wastewater treatment facilities, managing biological and chemical treatment of sewage for safe discharge.

WW Collection

Wastewater Collection Operators

Oversee and maintain the collection systems — pipes, lift stations, and infrastructure that move wastewater to treatment facilities.

Distribution

Water Distribution Operators

Manage the distribution network delivering treated water from storage to end users — mains, pressure zones, pumping stations.

Irrigator

Landscape Irrigators

Licensed by TCEQ to design, install, and maintain landscape irrigation systems in Texas. Required for commercial irrigation work.

Backflow

Backflow Prevention Assembly Testers

Certified to test and certify backflow prevention devices that protect the public water supply from contamination.

OSSF

On-Site Sewage Facility (OSSF)

Licensed to design, install, and maintain septic tanks and other on-site sewage systems for properties not connected to public sewer.

CS Inspector

Customer Service Inspectors

Perform field inspections on water service connections, meters, and related infrastructure for public water utilities.

Water Co.

Water Operations Company

Company registration for organizations that operate public water systems or provide water-related services under TCEQ oversight.

WTS

Water Treatment Specialists

Specialized license for professionals in commercial water treatment — including softening, filtration, and purification systems.

OSSF Maint.

OSSF Maintenance Providers

Licensed to perform ongoing maintenance, inspection, and repair of aerobic and other on-site sewage treatment systems.

LPST Mgr

LPST Project Managers

Licensed to manage remediation projects for leaking petroleum storage tanks (LPST), including corrective action planning.

Need Licensing Information?

All licensing is administered by the Texas Commission on Environmental Quality (TCEQ). Visit tceq.texas.gov/licensing for exam requirements, renewal information, and official records.
